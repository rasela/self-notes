Practical Field Guide — Steps & Affirmations

Prepare → Induce → Separate → Explore

The commitment — Buhlman's core instruction is a 30-day run: at least one technique every day, plus written goals. Stay calm with any sensations, stay fearless, and keep your attention off the physical body. Curiosity matters more than belief.

The Four Stages

What the experience looks like
1
Vibrational

Energy buzzing, humming or roaring sounds, numbness or temporary paralysis. This is your higher-frequency body shifting out of phase with the physical — not something to fear.

2
Separation

A distinct lifting, floating, or rolling out. Once you're clear of the body, the vibrations and sounds quickly fade.

3
Exploration

You exist independent of the body in a thought-responsive energy form. Movement is unlimited — walk, float, fly. Hold attention on the new environment to stay out.

4
Reentry

Simply thinking of the physical body pulls you back and reintegrates the two. Brief vibrations or numbness may recur, then fade.

Four Steps to Success

The in-the-moment discipline
1
Stay calmThe moment you notice any movement or shift, remain emotionally and mentally calm. Excitement or alarm causes an instant snap back into the body. Welcome the strangeness.
2
Expand the sensationsWhatever you feel — buzzing, vibration, energy — encourage it to spread through your whole being. Enjoy the higher vibratory rate rather than resisting it.
3
Direct away from the bodyUse a simple inner command that moves you off the body: "I float up and away," "I move to the next room." Never think about or reference your physical body.
4
Keep attention away from the bodyGet absorbed in the new environment. Duration depends entirely on focus — any thought of the physical body snaps you back.

Vibrational Signals

Inner signs separation is near

Learn to recognize these as a welcome prelude, not a disturbance. They often begin at the back of the neck and spread. Most people feel them while falling asleep, dreaming, or waking — and dismiss them. Don't.

Buzzing / humming / roaring Tingling or energy surges Voices or your name called Heaviness or sinking Numbness or paralysis Spreading lightness Electrical sense of energy Footsteps / a presence Internal rocking or spinning A limb lifting in sleep Wind, engine, bells, music

How to respond — Stay still and calm. Encourage the vibrations to spread. Picture yourself moving away ("Now I move to the door"). After full separation the vibrations drop — then hold attention away from the body.

Induction Techniques

Pick one and run it daily

Choose the one that fits you and stay with it — repetition is what makes any of these work. If visualization is hard for you, skip straight to the Early Morning and Sound methods; neither requires it.

◆
Target TechniquePick three vivid objects in one room (not your practice room). Physically walk to each, studying colour, texture, weight, light. Repeat the walk daily until you can recall every detail with eyes closed — then run the walk mentally as you drift to sleep. ~20 min. Builds the focus and visualization separation depends on.
◆
Mirror TechniqueStand before a full-length mirror and memorize your reflection objectively. Eyes closed, picture that self standing across the room; slowly animate it — fingers, arms, legs — then have it stand, walk, and open its eyes. Transfer your senses into the image and let the body fall asleep.
◆
Hypnagogic DriftAs you fall asleep, set the goal "I remain aware as I drift to sleep." Watch the imagery that appears, then gently shape it — picture yourself rising up and out (balloon, elevator, cloud). Repeat "Now I'm out-of-body" while staying calm and off the physical body.
◆
Early Morning Technique — the strongestSet an alarm for ~3–4 hours of sleep. Wake and move to a sofa or recliner. Relax and run your affirmations (aloud, then silent), building emotional intensity, making them your very last thought as you drift off. The mind-awake / body-relaxed state after partial sleep is the ideal launch point. No visualization needed.
◆
Dream ConversionUse recurring dream signals (flying, falling, vivid lucidity) as triggers. When you notice one, convert it: state your intent, demand "Clarity now!", and move away from any sense of the sleeping body. Affirmations and goal-setting beforehand prime this.
◆
Sound (OM) TechniqueRelax and focus just above the crown until body sensation fades. Intone OM seven times aloud, then seven times mentally, letting it resonate up and out of the head. Merge your awareness with the rising sound; let the body sleep while you ride the sound upward.
◆
Self-HypnosisIn deep relaxation, give yourself suggestions to separate consciously after the body sleeps. Buhlman provides a full script; the essence is repeated, present-tense commands toward conscious separation, delivered in the relaxed state.

Affirmations

Present tense · felt · repeated to sleep

Use them silently, building emotional and intellectual intensity in the last moments before sleep. Treat each as a firm commitment you fully expect to happen now — and feel completely open to receiving it.

Written goals — Buhlman credits written goals as the single biggest factor in his progress. Each night before bed, write your goal ~20 times (e.g. "Now I have a fully conscious out-of-body experience"). They surface at the right moment once you're out.
