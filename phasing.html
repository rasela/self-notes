Phasing
The Kepple Path

Shift focus — don't separate

Quiet → Notice → 3D Blackness → Step Through

A separate track — this is not the energy-body / vibrational / separation approach (Monroe's Journeys, Bruce's program, Buhlman). Phasing comes at the non-physical from a different angle and deliberately bypasses the vibrational-state and separation steps that are the bottleneck in the other track. Cross-reference only — don't blend the protocols.

What Phasing Is

Shift focus, not location

Phasing = shifting the focus of awareness from the physical to the non-physical while staying consciously awake — with no "exit" and no "leaving the body."

Kepple's framing (he and Monroe were both electronics engineers): two voltages can share one wire, mixed yet separated by their phase-angle relationship. Physical and non-physical consciousness occupy the same space, separated by a ~180° phase. Projection is "switching phase" between the two — not travelling anywhere.

Before the shift, the physical is the foreground and the non-physical sits in the background. After the 180° turn, the non-physical becomes the foreground and the physical recedes. You're not aware of the body — you've phased away from it, not climbed out of it.

Why this matters for me — the whole "get the buzz reliable → don't tense → separate the double" problem doesn't exist in this model. There's nothing to exit. For a tension / active-mind blocker at the separation threshold, a method with no separation threshold is the entire point.

The Continuum

Set by awareness, not location
Dream
Don't know you're non-physical — a normal dream.
Lucid
Know you're non-physical, but that's all.
Astral ★
Non-physical and full waking awareness — "the holy grail."

awareness slides both ways, mid-experience

The label is set by how aware you are, not where you are. Dream, lucid dream and OBE are one experience at different awareness levels — "an astral projection is just a dream where you have full waking awareness."

Two-question diagnostic

Realised you were dreaming? + Had full waking awareness?
No + Yes, or No + No → Dream awareness (incl. false awakening)
Yes + No → Lucid awareness
Yes + Yes → Astral awareness (= "astral projection")

Direct relevance — I already get spontaneous experiences. Under this model many were probably lucid-awareness ones I didn't ratchet up to astral. The work isn't "build from zero" — it's upgrade awareness in experiences I'm already having (see the conversion technique).

Focus Model

Monroe vs Kepple, mapped across

Kepple compressed Monroe's ~17 levels into a coarser Primary Focus (PF) model of 4 areas, plus a jump-off point he labelled Fz (= Monroe's Focus 21). The numbers are "motorway signposts," not destinations — you flow through them, you don't sit at them.

C1 / Focus 1PF1

Normal waking, open to the physical.

Focus 3→ PF2

Edge state, just before things "get lively."

Focus 10early PF2

Mind awake / body asleep. Like being engrossed in a TV movie — no longer aware of room or body. Blackness shows patches "lighter than black" coming and going.

Focus 12PF2 core

Hypnagogic imagery — velvety/liquid blackness, outline shapes, faces, swirling foggy colour. A sense of forward mental movement begins.

Focus 21 / "Fz"PF2 ↔ PF3

The 3D Blackness — often a starscape. The jump-off doorway. (Void / Point Consciousness / Fz = same place.)

Focus 22–27PF3

Afterlife / "ring" realms — the Astral proper.

Takeaway — don't collect Focus numbers; people fail by trying to "sit at F10 wondering what to do next." The flow is continuous: relaxed launch (F10) → imagery (F12) → forward movement → 3D Blackness (Fz/F21) → step through. The whole skill is slowing the flow down enough to control it — not forcing it.

The Techniques

Noticing is the on-ramp; Rundown is the workhorse
ANoticing — Kepple's core method

Stare into the 3D Blackness behind closed eyes and passively notice the faintest changes — a patch less black, a flash, a swirl, a sound. Don't analyse, don't grab. Each thing noticed builds fixation inward; the build-up snowballs until consciousness "pops" into the non-physical.

→
The paradoxNoticing is something you actively do, but you stay passively aware while doing it. Active attention, passive grip.
→
It's a build-up, not a checklistEach notice deepens the last — not "a thing… and another thing."
→
Paper drill (daytime)Dot on a white page, soft gaze at it, then notice every speck/crease in peripheral vision without moving the eyes. Carry that stance to the blackness.
→
Who it's forThe complete-beginner drill — it just teaches you to look within instead of exteriorising. Then Kepple moves people to Mental Rundown.
The biggest Noticing mechanic — look THROUGH the images

When imagery appears it sits right in your face, in the foreground — and the instinct is to watch it. Watching cues the physical eyes to "snatch a glance" (zaps you back) and helps you fall asleep. Instead, pitch focus beyond / behind the imagery, into the background, and hold it there as the body sleeps — you may "suddenly find yourself in Fz." The images are boundary-layer effects; the place you want is past the boundary. Don't camp on it.

Eye-focus-shift drill — the core skill is getting mental focus off the backs of the physical eyes. Picture a shape in mind → shift focus to "the backs of your closed eyes" → shift back to the shape. You'll feel a distinct click. Kepple aims focus up and back ~100° from horizontal — just past vertical, within the mind. Catch: relaxing the eyes is also the body's sleep cue, so relax them without following into sleep.

BA twist on Noticing — Tasker

Instead of passively observing, actively let patterns form — allow shapes to coalesce until you recognise something, then focus to strengthen it (it grows, or fades and you restart). Stay passive-but-focused; use Intent to make it move (raise an arm, roll a ball). Same goal: awareness fully parked in the blackness.

CMental Rundown — the workhorse

Run an imagined multi-sensory scene on repeat until consciousness "steps into" it in full 3D. The scene is a primer you shift into.

→
Two formsA short repeating scene (the classic trampoline bounce — feel the air, the backyard sounds) or a longer scripted scene.
→
Under-build itThe less you specify, the more your mind auto-fills (birdsong, waves) and the more it entices you in. Don't over-detail.
→
Allow, don't forceThe rundown just gives consciousness something more enticing than physical sense-input to shift toward.
→
Kepple's favouritesA rising-trot on horseback (engaging but not too engaging — plain walking isn't enough); or sitting in a small audience watching Monroe deliver the Gateway instructions.

Gateway tie-in — Astral Pulse practitioners ran the longer rundown alongside Monroe's Gateway Wave 1, Track 2 — it slots into existing Gateway audio practice without conflict. Kepple used the Wave-1 CD as a mental primer before a rundown.

The 3D Blackness is FOUND, not created

A trap carried over from traditional OBE: people try to manufacture the 3D Blackness (like visualising a rope). Wrong. It's already there inside you — you reach within and focus on it. Where? Look out horizontally, close the eyes (that's 2D blackness on the lids), then turn mental focus ~100° up from horizontal — within the mind, just past vertical. That's where it lives.

DEyes-Closed / Body-Still (EC/BS)

On waking (esp. from a lucid/astral experience), don't open your eyes, don't move a muscle. Lie still and you can slip straight back into what you just left — Tasker chained 4–6 in a row this way. Can be trained from cold sleep via a nightly affirmation to wake slowly and stay still. Hardest part: recognising the moment of waking and staying motionless.

ELucid → Astral conversion (the ratchet)

When lucid, stop, focus on yourself or the environment, and ask sincerely:

1
"Where am I?"
2
"Where am I going?"
3
"How did I get here?"
4
"What am I doing?"

Asked honestly in the non-physical, these snap you to full waking = astral awareness. Tasker says it has never failed for him; the only trick is remembering. If the questions don't fire, just hold the lucid state with pre-loaded fixed goals (meet a guide, go somewhere) repeated while falling asleep, so you act on them even at lower awareness.

The Vibrational Bridge

Where this track touches my other one
Why this section exists

Phasing is supposed to bypass vibrations — but Kepple had both modes, and his account of the vibrational route is the best description I've found of the exact state I get stuck in. Read it as cross-calibration, not as merging protocols.

Kepple's two exit modes

Step-in projectionpure phasing

Activate the "Astral Visual Interface," watch imagery on the screen, mentally step into it. No vibrations at all.

Travelling projectionvibrational

Vibrations begin, a "gateway" opens, he flies off through the RTZ into the Astral. His old "cannonball exit," later controlled.

His vibrational phenomenology (matches my buzzing/roar)

Onset begins at the crown / top of head every time — a "gateway" feeling.
Earliest sign: a gentle "swoosh" in the head ~every 30s, speeding to every 1–2s, then a low-frequency vibration like the brain shaking side to side.
He feels that swoosh ~half the time while naturally waking — most people dismiss it as "just waking." (Watch for it on WBTB.)
Vibrations can be heard as well as felt — different frequencies you can "tune in" to before take-off.

The control method — directly on my tension problem

"It is not enough merely to feel vibrations. You have to let them build in intensity." He first ramped intensity with slow deep breathing (not literal breath — it feels like breathing), later just by thinking about them. At sufficient intensity, separation is automatic — "you just know you can separate… you don't try to do anything, you just do it."

Posture fork: build vibrations while rocking → roll out into the real-time zone. Build to screaming intensity lying perfectly still → take off "like a cannonball" into the Astral.
99.9% of early failures = fear-induced tension — a deep fight/flight reflex, even when he welcomed it. Fix: familiarity through repetition.

Cross-calibration for my main track — (1) my "spread through the body" habit = Kepple's "bathe in them," confirmed good. (2) The missing piece may be building intensity, not just reaching the buzz — try the slow-deep-(felt)-breathing ramp. (3) Lie still vs rock is a deliberate fork: still → Astral, rock → RTZ. (4) "Snap awake" = the protective reflex, not a technique error — fix is repetition. (5) Watch for the swoosh on waking as a free recognition rep.

The Two Problems

The eyes & body dissociation
The Eyes — the #1 hurdle

Your focal point of consciousness normally "lives behind the physical eyes." The instant something definite appears in the blackness, the protective awareness shouts "What's that!" and the eyes dart to look — zapping you back to the body.

Fix — practice until the protective awareness treats everything as normal and the eyes stay switched-off. Never fully eradicated (it still got Kepple), but it fades hard with repetition.

Physical body dissociation

Phasing has two halves: keep consciousness fixed on something not-here, and reduce physical sensory input so the body stops anchoring you.

Sight — desensitises the moment eyes close.
Touch — keep perfectly still → input gets ignored → the numb/heavy sensation.
Hearing — hardest; use earplugs / white noise / quiet room.
Taste & smell — non-issues.

Divergence from the other track — Tasker concludes deep relaxation is not a primary requirement — only enough to remove muscle tension. Comfort + stillness + sensory reduction matter more than the deep energy-body relaxation the Monroe/Bruce track drills.

Staying In

Flood the non-physical senses

To keep from snapping back: examine the environment in minute detail — look hard, touch, listen, smell, even taste, intensely and with focus. This drowns out returning physical-sense input and locks you in (the inverse of waking someone by shaking them). Do it every few minutes, proactively — don't wait for the "about to return" feeling, which is usually too late (if it hits, fall back to EC/BS).

Perception caveat — the non-physical is malleable to expectation. A snap judgment ("that's a dead squirrel") becomes your reality and won't self-correct. Unfamiliar things render as the closest match your mind can find (unknown creature → "dragon"). What you experience is real; how it appears is a metaphor. Don't make snap judgments; control emotions — a sad snap-judgment can spiral and end the whole scene.

When & Meditation

Best window + the prerequisite
After-sleep exiteasiest

Practice right after waking from sleep/nap — body relaxed, mind on the groggy-but-aware edge. This is my WBTB window, different vocabulary.

Conscious exitharder

From full waking, any time — requires meditation skill to manufacture the primed state. Where most beginners fail by jumping straight in.

Phasing rests on being able to quiet inner dialogue. Two standard anchors: a meaningless mantra repeated internally, or a simple visual object (candle, geometric shape) held in the blackness. When focus drifts, gently return to the anchor. Eventually focus sticks → strange effects appear (time distortion, the physical melting into a "sea of nothing" = the Void / Point Consciousness = you've phased).

Lineage & Reception

Who said it + how it holds up
Frank Kepple

Posted thousands of times on the Astral Pulse forum (~early 2000s); no books. Developed the phasing method and the 4-level Focus Model. Fell ill ~2005, presumed deceased. Compiled posthumously into the Frank Kepple Phasing Resource.

Ryan Tasker (Xanth)

Distilled Kepple + his own practice into the free eBook My Astral Projection Truth (2011). Strips the mystical framing and says not to over-trust any author including himself — treat as single-practitioner, hold the metaphysics lightly.

Adjacent lineage

Connects to Bruce Moen ("Moen-school" of phasing) and, loosely, Castaneda's "assemblage point" as a metaphor for the phase shift (speculative).

What holds up (practitioner praise)

"Years on exit techniques with no success, then Noticing worked." The single strongest recurring pattern.
Lower fear/anxiety — no vibrations, sounds or separation jolt = fewer abort-spikes. Attractive when the blocker is the startle reflex (me).
The "it's all one thing" reframe relieves pressure — people stop feeling they "failed" at only lucid, which helps them progress.

What to weigh (criticism)

"Just lucid dreaming with a fancy name." Even some believers (Erin Pavlina) argue a true OBE needs felt separation — phasing is contested as "not real OBE" inside the AP community.
Imagination-vs-reality — the Rundown begins in imagination; hard to tell a real shift from an absorbing daydream.
Neuroscience baseline — OBE/AP read as sleep-wake boundary phenomena. The techniques fit cleanly; the metaphysics is where it departs.
Correction — the two tracks aren't rivals

The forum's better conclusion: they're two ends of one process. A classic separation OBE = a phasing run you interrupt at F10/12 to fire an exit; a phasing run = a classic attempt where you skip the exit and keep going. Same continuum from different ends — I can fork mid-session.
