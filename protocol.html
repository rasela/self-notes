The Two Vectors

Engine & Accelerant — different rhythms

The Engine is Focus 10 mastery — it carries no sleep cost and is the real long game. The Accelerant is WBTB, clustered into short blocks: powerful, but it fragments sleep, so it's used periodically and then put down. Block-and-rest beats nightly grinding, which quietly eats itself.

Engine week — Focus 10, full rest
Accelerant week — WBTB intensive

Vector 1 — The Engine

Focus 10 Deepening

Daily practice from a full night's sleep or a morning session. The goal: reach the vibration state with zero sleep cost. Slow to build, but it's the engine you don't want to burn. Spend the bulk of your time here.

Vector 2 — The Accelerant

WBTB Intensive

Wake in the late, REM-dense window, stay up briefly, then re-enter sleep with awareness intact — dropping you near-straight into a lucid foothold. Run in short blocks, then stop completely and let the system reset.

WBTB, in one breath — Sleep ~5 hours. Wake fully (alarm), stay up 20–30 minutes with low light and a quiet intention to stay aware. Lie back down and let sleep return while holding a thread of awareness. You'll re-enter REM with a lucid opening.

The Four Weeks

Tap a night to mark it done
Week 1 Build the Engine No sleep cost

Lay the foundation. Every session from full rest — no WBTB this week. You're training the "mind awake, body asleep" line until you can find it reliably.

Sessions 1–2 · Introduction to Focus 10Run the intro tape. Goal is full relaxation and finding the threshold — don't chase anything beyond it yet.
Sessions 3–4 · Advanced Focus 10Push past relaxation toward genuine "body asleep." Watch for the physical markers — heaviness, skin sensitivity, phantom tones.
Session 5 · Free Flow 10Practise holding and moving within the state once it's established.
One tape-free entry · 4-7-8 breathingTry reaching Focus 10 without audio. This is what real, tape-independent mastery feels like.
Week 2 First WBTB Block 4–5 nights, clustered

Cluster the intensive into consecutive nights — only before light next days. After the block, stop completely for the rest of the week.

Night 1 · Establish the rhythmSleep 5h → wake 20–30 min → re-enter with the intention to stay aware.
Night 2 · Hold the transitionSame pattern. Practise noticing the falling/heaviness sensation instead of losing it.
Night 3 · Anchor at the crownOn re-entry, place attention at the top of the head and let the vibrations rise rather than forcing them.
Night 4 · Lengthen the exhale4 in · hold 5 · exhale 6–7 · hold out. The longer exhale shifts you toward the sleep-breathing state.
Night 5 (optional) · Final push, then stopIf you're not sleep-debted, run one more — then close the block and let the nervous system recover.
Week 3 Recover & Deepen Sleep debt resets

Back to full sleep — no WBTB. Let recovery do its work while you keep deepening the engine, now aiming for the vibration state from full rest.

Daily · Focus 10 from morning restAim for the vibration state without fragmenting sleep. Spend ~60% of practice time holding true Focus 10.
As needed · Release & RechargeIf you feel blocked or physically tense, run Tape 3 — tension itself blocks the vibration state.
Practise fractioningEnter Focus 10, come out without fully waking, re-enter. Cycling builds mastery faster than holding once.
One tape-free vibration attemptCrown focus + long exhale, no audio. Drop expectation — wanting it tightens you and blocks the let-go.
Week 4 Refined WBTB Block 4 nights, then full reset

A second clustered intensive — now with the in-the-moment cues sharp. After this block, stop for ~2 weeks and let everything reset before the next cycle.

Night 1 · Re-enter the rhythm5h sleep → 20–30 min up → re-enter. Carry the cue cards below into the moment.
Night 2 · Intensify the floatWhen lucid and floating, do not take off. Anchor to the body first.
Night 3 · Convert float into separationLock stable presence — rub dream hands, look toward your sleeping form — before exploring.
Night 4 · Final push, then full stopClose the block. Two weeks off WBTB; keep the engine running on full-rest Focus 10.

In the Moment

Cue cards for the threshold

Cue 1

Intensify the float, don't fly. When you're lucid and floating again, the reflex to take off and explore usually collapses the state. Stay. Deepen first.

Cue 2

Anchor to the body. Feel for your body in the bed, look toward your sleeping form, rub your dream hands together, or fixate on a single detail to lock presence.

Cue 3

Separation before exploration. Convert the float into stable separation first. Explore second — only once presence holds.

Cue 4

Drop the chase. Wanting it tightens you and blocks the let-go. The breakthroughs that arrive "after they gave up chasing" are describing the relaxation that returns once the pressure comes off.

Guardrails — non-negotiable. Only run WBTB before a genuinely light next day. Cap each block at 4–5 nights. Skip any WBTB night when you're already sleep-debted. With two young kids and live trading, chronic sleep fragmentation taxes the exact faculties you can least afford to blur — a tired parent and a tired trader make the same reactive, low-patience mistakes. Periodic and protected, never frequent and indefinite.

Journal Prompt

Capture it before it fades

Post-Session Capture

  • Depth rating (1–10) — how deep did the state go?
  • Physical markers — tingling, temperature shifts, heaviness or lightness, skin sensitivity, phantom tones.
  • Sounds & images — anything seen or heard at the threshold.
  • Emotional tone — calm, anxious, neutral, exhilarated.
  • Vibration / float / separation — did any emerge? What collapsed it?
  • Daily-life echoes — anything unusual in the days that follow.
