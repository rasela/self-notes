01 / 09

What absorption actually is

Absorption happens when aggressive market orders hit passive limit orders, but price fails to continue in the direction of that aggression. The intent shows up on the tape — the result doesn't show up on the chart.

↑ Buy-side aggression

Aggressive buyers lift the offer, but price cannot move higher.

Result → passive sellers absorbing

↓ Sell-side aggression

Aggressive sellers hit the bid, but price cannot move lower.

Result → passive buyers absorbing

// Main takeaway

Absorption = high effort with low result.

Reading it

Heavy ask volume stacks into the highs (847, 612, 389…) and cumulative delta reads strongly positive (+2,004 of buy aggression) — yet the candle prints a failed high with a long wick. Maximum effort, no follow-through on price.

02 / 09

Aggressive vs passive participants

Every print is a transaction between two sides — but only one of them is visible on the tape. Reading absorption means inferring what you can't see.

// Aggressive — the visible side

The visible side

  • Market orders
  • Cross the spread
  • Show up clearly in footprint volume, delta, and imbalances

// Passive — the inferred side

The inferred side

  • Limit orders
  • Provide liquidity
  • Absorption is inferred when price fails to move despite aggression

Note

Not all passive participation is fully visible. Icebergs and hidden liquidity can absorb aggression without appearing completely on the DOM.

03 / 09

The effort vs result framework

Every candle is a relationship between two variables: how hard someone tried, and what they got for it. Map the relationship and the chart starts telling you something different.

Low effort · High result

Thin liquidity

Book sweep — price moved easily because there was nothing in the way.

High effort · High result

Initiative continuation

Aggression rewarded — pressure converted into directional movement.

Low effort · Low result

Exhaustion

No real participation — the auction simply ran out of fuel.

High effort · Low result

Absorption

Real pressure meeting real passive liquidity. Someone is actively defending the level.

04 / 09

How absorption appears on the footprint

Absorption isn't a single print — it's a pattern of relationships across volume, delta, imbalance, and price behavior. Look for all of these together.

Signature on the tape

Heavy volume at a level
Strong positive or negative delta
Aggressive imbalances into the level
Repeated attempts to push through
Price fails to expand
Wick forms at the level
POC remains near the rejection area
Next candle fails to continue

! One print is not enough

Read the relationship between pressure and outcome — not any single number in isolation.

// Synthesis

Bullish effort → bearish result.

05 / 09

Why absorption alone is not a trade signal

Absorption happens constantly throughout the chart. Most of it is noise. Absorption only deserves weight when it happens at a meaningful location. Context is the filter that separates a tradeable read from a coincidence.

01Prior high or low
02Session high or low
03Premium / discount zone
04Higher-timeframe level
05Liquidity sweep
06Gamma level
07Call wall / put wall
08FVG / imbalance zone
09End of a completed move

// Main takeaway

Location determines whether absorption matters.

06 / 09

Why absorption can lead to reversals

When an aggressive push gets absorbed at a level, the participants who chased that move are now offside. Their forced exits become fuel for the opposite direction.

Stage 01

Breakout attempt

Stage 02

Aggressive buyers chase

Stage 03

Passive sellers absorb

Stage 04

No acceptance at the level

Stage 05

Late buyers trapped

Stage 06

Pressure flips lower

Upside momentumReversal fuel

// Why it matters

Absorption often matters because trapped participants can fuel the move in the opposite direction.

07 / 09

What confirmation looks like after absorption

Absorption is evidence. Confirmation determines whether it becomes a trade idea. You don't trade the wall — you trade what happens once the wall holds. Absorption without confirmation can still resolve into continuation.

Aggression into level
No result
Absorption suspected
↓
Wait for confirmation
Delta flip · close away · pressure shift
Trade idea valid

Confirmation signals

Delta flips direction
Candle closes away from absorbed level
Next candle fails to continue
Orderflow pressure begins to shift
Price accepts back inside prior range
Opposite-side liquidity becomes target

! Risk

Absorption can still fail. Strong initiative activity can re-assert itself and continue straight through the level.

! In chop

If this occurs in the middle of chop or balance with no meaningful location, the correct decision is usually no trade.

09 / 09 · Closing

The real edge

The edge is not spotting absorption. The edge is knowing when absorption matters, where it is happening, and whether the market confirms that aggression has failed.
