OBE Technique
Taxonomy

A staged map — by step, not by author

Relax → Raise → Vibrate → Separate

The one principle — across all three authors: passivity. The state collapses the instant effort becomes muscular. The natural reflex to vibrations, noises or falling is to brace — train yourself out of it. Even visualization can leak into physical effort and break the exit.

Stage 0 — Relax / Quiet

Reach mind-awake / body-asleep

Goal: the threshold — Monroe's Focus 10, Bruce's trance state. Both my blockers (tension, active mind) live here.

Focus 10 cooldown tapeMonroe

Purpose-built for exactly my threshold. Use as the nightly anchor.

Deep physical relaxationBruce Wk1

Progressive, area-by-area. Learn it cold so it runs automatically. Don't do it overtired — you'll condition yourself to fall asleep.

Breath awarenessBruce Wk1

Focus on the leading edge of airflow — a focus object that quiets the mind without arousing it.

Counting breaths 1→12Bruce Wk2

Tethers the mind; same logic as "1, I'm still awake…".

WBTB timingSleep sci

Wake ~4.5–5 hrs, up 10–15 min, lie back down. My strongest lever — brain is primed to drop back fast, defusing the active-mind blocker.

Sit upright for warm-upBruce

No head support; slight discomfort prevents falling asleep during entrainment.

Tension fix here — release on the exhale only; do nothing on the inhale. Don't actively relax — just let each exhale drop you heavier. Breaks the bracing reflex before vibrations start.

Stage 1 — Raise / Loosen

Mobilise the energy body

Goal: make vibrations arrive reliably, not "time to time." This is the stage that most directly fixes my consistency gap. Mercer's vibrations appeared only after sustained daily energy-raising.

Body-awareness targetingBruce Wk1

Point of awareness on a body part. Foundation for everything; doubles as a mind-quieting focus object.

Stirring / brushing / wrappingBruce Wk1–4

Move awareness in circles/sweeps over a target to stimulate it.

Foot & sole stimulationBruce Wk1

Feet are main energy conduits; strong buzzing/heaviness here is expected and a good sign.

Leg/limb sponging & bounceBruce Wk1–2

Sweep awareness foot→hip→foot; speed up into a "bounce."

Spinal bounceBruce

Tailbone ↔ crown, ~½ sec each way. Breathe naturally — this one tends to cause breath-holding.

Primary-centre stimulationBruce Wk4

Base→crown vortexes. Avoid the genital centre during practice (distracting).

Energy raisingBruce/Mercer

Full-body energy loosening — the reported difference-maker for making vibrations show up at all.

My actual prescription — vibrations are intermittent because the energy body isn't consistently online. Daily Stage-1 work (esp. feet + energy raising) is the reliability fix. Bruce frames energy-body loosening as the final prep before any exit — build this layer before touching Stage 3.

Stage 2 — Vibrational State

Stabilise & amplify · my focus

Goal: reach buzzing/roar, then hold and intensify without grabbing. For the next stretch, stop here deliberately — reach the state, amplify it passively, no exit attempt. This trains it to become familiar and stable.

Entry methods — how to summon the vibrations

L-path reach-and-pullMonroe Ch16

Reach line-of-sight out ~1 ft → ~10 ft → bend 90° up into an L → vibrations arrive at the reach → pull down. Outward-first disengages the body before turning up, sidestepping my tension blocker structurally. My primary entry.

Whole-body spread / sweepMonroe / me

Once buzzing arrives, sweep head→toe, back and forth. My natural move — keep it.

Imagined rocking / spinningKinetic

Triggers vibrations without deep relaxation, bypassing the tension trap entirely. Worth testing.

WILD via WBTBLucid/OBE

Stay conscious as the body sleeps. Sleep-paralysis onset often is the vibrational state; my WBTB timing is ideal.

Hypnagogic anchoringClassic

Watch drifting images passively; keep a thin thread of awareness alive while the body shuts down.

Stabilise & amplify — the actual work

①
Do nothingWhen buzzing comes, take hands off the wheel. Any "trying" = tension.
②
Amplify passivelyInvite "more" while staying limp (Buhlman's intensify cue), OR spread head-to-toe (my gentler route). Pick whichever doesn't induce bracing.
③
Watch for the "click"Bruce's common separation signal. Stay relaxed, don't get excited — excitement blows it. Not exiting for now, so just note it and keep amplifying.

Failure-mode diagnostic — log each session

Snap awake → over-aroused; more Stage 0.
State fades → not enough energy-body charge; more Stage 1.
Pinned / stuck → that's pre-exit; correct response is passivity, not force.

Mercer's cautionary tale — he tried an exit cold, before ready, hit huge waves of energy, panicked, and developed a years-long gut-level abort reflex. Build the state to comfortable familiarity before asking it to do anything. Stage 2 only, for now.

Stage 3 — Separation

Defer — logged for later

Not yet — don't attempt separation until the vibrational state is reliable and calm. Attempting to leave reintroduces the exact tension that's breaking me.

Float-outBruce Wk7+

Simplest exit — hold the feeling of floating up and out, keep absolutely still.

Rope techniqueMonroe

Reach non-physical attention to a point above; pull/climb hand-over-hand.

Climbing down a rope/ladderBruce

Imagined climbing-down with limb body-awareness; also deepens trance.

Roll-out / sit-upClassic

Imagine rolling sideways or sitting up out of the body — no muscles.

Jump / blast-offBruce

Crouch-and-launch kinetic exit.

Affirmation + targetingBuhlman

At vibration peak, command movement toward a target point, not "leaving" — target focus bypasses body engagement.
