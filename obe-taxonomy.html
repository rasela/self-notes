OBE Technique
Taxonomy

A staged map — by step, not by author

Relax → Raise → Vibrate → Separate

The one principle — across all three authors: passivity. The state collapses the instant effort becomes muscular. The natural reflex to vibrations, noises or falling is to brace — train yourself out of it. Even visualization can leak into physical effort and break the exit.

Monroe's Fear Barrier — Monroe calls this the single greatest obstacle, present in everyone: blind, unreasoning fear that turns to panic at the vibrations and at separation. Its first face is the death fear ("get back in, you're dying!"). He only got past it by repeating the process 18–20 times until familiarity assuaged it. This is the same bracing reflex that breaks my exits — the fix is reps, not force. Pass it consciously and you've passed the milestone.

Stage 0 — Relax / Quiet

Reach mind-awake / body-asleep

Goal: the threshold — Monroe's Focus 10, Bruce's trance state. Both my blockers (tension, active mind) live here.

Focus 10 cooldown tapeMonroe

Purpose-built for exactly my threshold. Use as the nightly anchor.

Deep physical relaxationBruce Wk1

Progressive, area-by-area. Learn it cold so it runs automatically. Don't do it overtired — you'll condition yourself to fall asleep.

Breath awarenessBruce Wk1

Focus on the leading edge of airflow — a focus object that quiets the mind without arousing it.

Counting breaths 1→12Bruce Wk2

Tethers the mind; same logic as "1, I'm still awake…".

WBTB timingSleep sci

Wake ~4.5–5 hrs, up 10–15 min, lie back down. My strongest lever — brain is primed to drop back fast, defusing the active-mind blocker.

Sit upright for warm-upBruce

No head support; slight discomfort prevents falling asleep during entrainment.

Tension fix here — release on the exhale only; do nothing on the inhale. Don't actively relax — just let each exhale drop you heavier. Breaks the bracing reflex before vibrations start.

Monroe's borderland ladder — the graded entry

Borderland sleep stateMonroe Ch16

Monroe's preferred entry: lie down, take conscious awareness up to the edge between sleep and wakefulness, into it, and through it — without falling asleep. The whole skill is holding that delicate "edge." His four graded conditions:

A
Relaxation + the litanyBody and mind relaxed, free of any time-urgency. Eyes closed; breathe through a half-opened mouth.
B
Mind-pictures fade to blacknessHypnagogic images (from the day's visual concentration) drift in, then fade. Condition B = you can lie indefinitely, no nervousness, seeing only blackness.
C
Systematic deepeningLoosen the rigid hold on the borderland edge and drift deeper, a little each session. Senses shut down in order: touch → smell/taste → hearing → vision (last). Learn to go "down" to a level and return at will.
D
Condition C while fully restedReach C refreshed, not tired — this preserves conscious control. Best done immediately on waking from sleep/nap, before moving in bed, body still relaxed, mind alert. (Matches Gurriaran's "rested not tired" rule.)

Monroe on drugs — he tried and rejected drug-induced relaxation early: barbiturates / tranquillizers / alcohol all buy relaxation at the cost of conscious control and distorted perception. Noted only so the option is closed, not open.

Monroe's setup for the vibrational attempt — Lie on a north–south axis, head to magnetic north. Loosen clothing; remove metal/jewellery touching skin. Keep covered so you're slightly warmer than comfortable. Darken the room so no light shows through the eyelids — but not fully blacked out (you need a visual point of reference). Guarantee no interruptions; set no time limit. Then the relaxation litany, said mentally 5×: an intent to perceive, remember, and recall only what's beneficial.

Stage 1 — Raise / Loosen

Mobilise the energy body

Goal: make vibrations arrive reliably, not "time to time." This is the stage that most directly fixes my consistency gap. Mercer's vibrations appeared only after sustained daily energy-raising.

Body-awareness targetingBruce Wk1

Point of awareness on a body part. Foundation for everything; doubles as a mind-quieting focus object.

Stirring / brushing / wrappingBruce Wk1–4

Move awareness in circles/sweeps over a target to stimulate it.

Foot & sole stimulationBruce Wk1

Feet are main energy conduits; strong buzzing/heaviness here is expected and a good sign.

Leg/limb sponging & bounceBruce Wk1–2

Sweep awareness foot→hip→foot; speed up into a "bounce."

Spinal bounceBruce

Tailbone ↔ crown, ~½ sec each way. Breathe naturally — this one tends to cause breath-holding.

Primary-centre stimulationBruce Wk4

Base→crown vortexes. Avoid the genital centre during practice (distracting).

Energy raisingBruce/Mercer

Full-body energy loosening — the reported difference-maker for making vibrations show up at all.

My actual prescription — vibrations are intermittent because the energy body isn't consistently online. Daily Stage-1 work (esp. feet + energy raising) is the reliability fix. Bruce frames energy-body loosening as the final prep before any exit — build this layer before touching Stage 3.

Stage 2 — Vibrational State

Stabilise & amplify · my focus

Goal: reach buzzing/roar, then hold and intensify without grabbing. For the next stretch, stop here deliberately — reach the state, amplify it passively, no exit attempt. This trains it to become familiar and stable.

Entry methods — how to summon the vibrations

L-path reach-and-pullMonroe Ch16

Reach line-of-sight out ~1 ft → ~10 ft → bend 90° up into an L → vibrations arrive at the reach → pull down. Outward-first disengages the body before turning up, sidestepping my tension blocker structurally. My primary entry.

Whole-body spread / sweepMonroe / me

Once buzzing arrives, sweep head→toe, back and forth. My natural move — keep it.

Imagined rocking / spinningKinetic

Triggers vibrations without deep relaxation, bypassing the tension trap entirely. Worth testing.

WILD via WBTBLucid/OBE

Stay conscious as the body sleeps. Sleep-paralysis onset often is the vibrational state; my WBTB timing is ideal.

Hypnagogic anchoringClassic

Watch drifting images passively; keep a thin thread of awareness alive while the body shuts down.

Stabilise & amplify — the actual work

①
Do nothingWhen buzzing comes, take hands off the wheel. Any "trying" = tension.
②
Amplify passivelyInvite "more" while staying limp (Buhlman's intensify cue), OR spread head-to-toe (my gentler route). Pick whichever doesn't induce bracing.
③
Watch for the "click"Bruce's common separation signal. Stay relaxed, don't get excited — excitement blows it. Not exiting for now, so just note it and keep amplifying.

Control the vibrations — Monroe's smoothing sequence

1
Acclimatize — do nothingWhen the waves (electric-shock-like, painless) permeate the body, lie quietly and analyse them objectively until they fade of their own accord — usually ~5 min. After several times you realize you're not being electrocuted. Don't struggle against the paralysis; you can break it by sitting up with force of will, but that defeats the point.
2
Manipulate — the head-to-toe sweepDirect the vibrations into a ring / force them into the head, then push them down to the toes and back. Set a rhythmic head→toe→head wave (~10 s round trip: 5 down, 5 back), give it momentum, then let it run until fade. (This is the "spread" I already do — confirmed as Monroe's own step.)
3
Smooth them — the speed-up (NEW for me)The vibrations feel "rough," shaking you at the molecular level. Smooth them by mentally "pulsing" them to a higher frequency (base rate ~27 cps). Responds slowly at first; success = vibrations no longer rough but a steady, solid effect.
4
Accelerate to disassociation pitchThe faster vibration is the form that permits separation. Once momentum is set, acceleration becomes automatic — frequency climbs "like a motor starting up" until imperceptible; sensation becomes mild body warmth + slight tingling. Consistently reaching this = ready for first separation.

The missing lever for me — my page stops at the head-to-toe spread. Monroe's smoothing/speed-up (steps 3–4) is the step beyond it: don't just sweep the buzz, pulse it faster until it goes smooth and warm. That smooth-fast state — not the rough onset — is what actually permits separation. Pairs with Kepple's "build intensity," reached differently.

Failure-mode diagnostic — log each session

Snap awake → over-aroused; more Stage 0.
State fades → not enough energy-body charge; more Stage 1.
Pinned / stuck → that's pre-exit; correct response is passivity, not force.

Mercer's cautionary tale — he tried an exit cold, before ready, hit huge waves of energy, panicked, and developed a years-long gut-level abort reflex. Build the state to comfortable familiarity before asking it to do anything. Stage 2 only, for now.

Monroe's Journeys Ch.17 exits — the core sequence

Thought-control prerequisite (Ch.17) — in the vibrational state you respond instantly to every thought, willful or stray. Before separating, get as close to "no thought" / single-thought concentration as possible — a single stray idea triggers an unwanted response (Monroe blamed this for his many unplanned trips). This is the gate to all three exits below.

① Release of extremitiesMonroe Ch17 · familiarization

The first, no-commitment step — proves the Second Body is real before any full exit. In the vibrational state, work one hand/arm at a time. Reach — outward in the direction the arm points (not up/down) — toward an object beyond physical reach. Feel it; then push fingertips through it (slight resistance, then it passes through) to a second object behind. Withdraw, return the limb to alignment. A variation: lift arms and clasp hands above the chest — with closed eyes you'll see both physical arms (at rest) and non-physical arms (raised). Always re-align non-physical limbs before shutting off the vibrations.

② Lift-outMonroe Ch17 · primary exit

Monroe's simplest separation and the one to try first. In the vibrational state, holding full thought-control: think of getting lighter, of floating upward — and how nice it would be to float up. The subjective "how nice" is essential — you respond emotionally, reacting in anticipation. Hold only these thoughts and you disassociate, floating gently up from the physical. Stay within ~3 ft, hovering; don't move laterally or further up yet. Return by simply thinking yourself back.

③ RotationMonroe Ch17 · primary exit

The second method — try after lift-out. Under the same conditions, slowly turn over as if rolling to get comfortable in bed. No arms/legs — start the twist from head and shoulders, slow and firm (too fast and you "spin like a log"). Friction-free, weightless turning tells you you're disassociating. Turn until you sense 180° (face-to-face with your physical body — two 90° turns, uncannily easy to sense), stop by thinking it, then think of floating up and backing away. Of the two, try lift-out first, then settle on whichever is easier for you.

First sessions — stay within 3 ft of the physical, vision zero (eyes stay closed), do nothing but practice getting "out" and back for the first 3–4 exercises. Return = just "think" yourself back into alignment. Keep it close and familiar before any real travel — wild early trips force you to re-learn what you've gained.
