How It Works

Why wired · why one state at a time

A binaural beat doesn't exist in the audio at all. The left ear gets one pure tone (the carrier, e.g. 200 Hz) and the right ear gets a slightly different one (e.g. 208 Hz). Your brainstem combines them and perceives the difference — an 8 Hz pulse — which the brain tends to entrain toward (the frequency-following response).

That's why the player hard-pans each oscillator fully left and right, and why wired stereo headphones matter: Bluetooth codecs can down-mix or desync the channels, at which point both ears hear both tones and the beat collapses into ordinary audio.

Carrier choice is mostly comfort — lower carriers tend to give a stronger, smoother beat percept than high ones. The fine-tune slider goes down to 40 Hz: below that, most headphones stop reproducing a pure sine cleanly and the carrier starts crowding the beat frequency itself, so the tone fades out before the beat does.

In Journey mode the player walks through stages hands-free — each transition glides the beat frequency over 15 seconds instead of jumping, and can end with a gentle fade to silence so the session doesn't snap you out of state. Journeys you build can be saved on-device.

While playing, the player holds a wake lock so the screen stays on by itself — don't switch the screen off manually. Browsers run inside the OS audio policy, and a screen turned off by hand can suspend the audio engine mid-session (the status line under the timer shows whether the lock is active). Just dim the brightness, lay the phone face-up, and let the player manage the screen.
