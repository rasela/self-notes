How to read it

Strategy → Optimal Risk

Enter your real win rate and R:R, then a drawdown you can stomach. The tool binary-searches the risk-per-trade fraction whose 95th-percentile simulated max drawdown just touches your limit — that's your Optimal Risk. Hit Apply to push it into the sizing tab.

Sizing → Contracts

Risk % × account = dollars at risk. Divide by (stop distance × $ per point) and you get the contract count, rounded for the order ticket. The $/point presets cover gold and Nasdaq futures.

Simulation → The fan

The equity curve is a Monte Carlo fan of runs using the applied risk size. Green is the best path, red the worst, the shaded band is the 95% confidence area. The spread multiple shows how far best and worst diverge — a wide fan means the same edge can still hand you very different outcomes.

Read me first

This is a planning toy, not a guarantee. Expectancy and profit factor are exact from your inputs; risk of ruin is an approximation and the sim assumes fixed-fractional compounding with i.i.d. trades — real returns are streaky and correlated. Garbage-in win rate ⇒ garbage-out size. Use it to compare sizing choices, not to predict your account.
