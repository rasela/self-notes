Gateway Tapes — Focus 10 Protocol

Breathing → ECB → Affirmation → Tuning → REBAL

Before every session — Use the bathroom. Loosen tight clothing, remove shoes and contacts. Lie down or sit with head supported. Darken the room. Allow 30–45 min uninterrupted. Wait at least 1 hour after eating. Avoid alcohol or excessive caffeine — they limit the effectiveness of Hemi-Sync® signals.

Opening Sequence

Resonant Breathing

Step 1 — Energize Your System

Inhale deeply, pulling vibrant energy from all parts of your physical body up into your head. Hold for a comfortable length of time — eventually building to a count of 10 — swirling the energy around your brain. Exhale slowly, as if blowing out a candle, imagining stale energy expelled through the soles of your feet.

Repeat 6 times, systematically filling from head to feet on successive inhales — like filling an inverted jar from top to bottom. First breath fills the brain; each successive breath brings energy progressively lower: upper chest → abdomen → pelvis → thighs → knees → lower legs and feet.

Energy Conversion Box

ECB — Step 2 — Clear Your Mental Space

See, feel, hear, or simply imagine your personal container. It can be anything — a simple wooden chest, a vacuum cleaner, a nuclear particle collector. Place into it all concerns, worries, obligations, and distractions for the duration of the exercise.

Use concrete symbols: a wallet for financial worries, a photo for a person on your mind, the word "can't" for a limiting belief, a desk for work obligations. The container converts your scattered energy into usable power.

💡 Tip — You can return to your ECB at any point during an exercise to deposit new distractions as they arise. Allow it to change form as needed — it is yours. If you naturally leave distractions behind, you may not need this much at all.

The Gateway Affirmation

Step 3 — Set Your Intention
I am more than my physical body. Because I am more than physical matter, I can perceive that which is greater than the physical world. Therefore, I deeply desire to Expand, to Experience; to Know, to Understand; to Control, to Use such greater energies and energy systems as may be beneficial and constructive to me and to those who follow me. Also, I deeply desire the help and cooperation, the assistance, the understanding of those individuals whose wisdom, development, and experience are equal to or greater than my own. I ask their guidance and protection from any influence or any source that might provide me with less than my stated desires.

Note — You may use the words as spoken on the tape, modify them for personal relevance, or substitute your own version entirely. What matters is the feeling and intent — not the words themselves. Experiment. Make it yours.

Resonant Tuning

RT — Step 4 — Vocalize to Activate

Follow the tones on the tape and vocalize aloud. The sounds accelerate the gathering of your vibrational energy, reduce internal dialogue, vitalize your entire system, and loosen blockages in natural energy channels.

Notice any physical manifestations as you vocalize — tingling, warmth, vibration in the chest or skull. The sounds on the recording are only a guide: set your own rhythm and pace, move up and down the scale, experiment with different vowel sounds.

💡 Tip — The opening and closing of your eyes during this step may feel awkward at first — try it anyway. It's a valuable first step in learning that you can control habitual and autonomic processes.

Resonant Energy Balloon

REBAL — Step 5 — Protective Field & Magnetic Attractor

Your REBAL is an intensification of your own energy field — not a static bubble, but a breath-driven flow. Operating on a principle of resonance, it acts as a sieve: only energy equal to or of a higher vibration than yours can access your field. It also acts as a magnet, attracting influences and guidance from higher energy forms.

→
Take a Resonant Energy BreathInhale vibrant energy from all around you. As you hold the breath, think of a bright moving circle with the number 10 inside it. Feel energy gathering and intensifying.
→
Exhale — Let It Surround YouAs you exhale, let the circle expand outward until it completely surrounds your body in all directions — above, below, front, back, sides. Feel its membrane form around you.
→
Shift into a Spiral FlowLet energy flow out of the top of your head like a fountain, continuing gently down and around you, re-entering through the soles of your feet. Then shift this into a spiral — winding down around you and coiling back up inside.
→
Sense Its QualitiesYour REBAL may spontaneously change form — develop a delicate membrane, become radiant, or feel like a charged battery. It may be invisible to you at first. That's fine. Sense it through feeling.
→

Note — The REBAL is a breath-driven energy flow — not a static bubble you visualize around yourself. That distinction matters in practice.

The Vibration State

Gateway to Out-of-Body

The vibration state is a well-documented threshold to the out-of-body experience — and the same buzzing, full-body energetic resonance reported on DMT. If you've felt it there, you already have a reference point: the goal is to recognize that familiar "somewhere between" state and stay present with it rather than letting it pass.

Note — Vibrations typically emerge only after Focus 10 is properly reached and stabilized. Don't chase the vibration directly — build the state it lives inside.

1
Master Focus 10 first — "mind awake, body asleep," not just relaxedMost people stall here: they reach relaxation but never the actual "body asleep" paralysis that signals true Focus 10. Treat it literally — mind fully awake, body genuinely switched off. The vibrations live on the far side of that line. Spend ~60% of your practice time here if vibrations and OBE are the goal.
2
The arm-drop technique — catch the thresholdLie on your back before sleep, arms bent 90°, pointing at the ceiling. As you drift, the arms drop and snap your awareness back — let you notice the subtle falling/heaviness sensation of the transition. Practise extending that feeling instead of losing it. (Edison used dropping balls for the same liminal state.)
3
Focus awareness at the crownPlace the point of attention at the top of the head (crown chakra) and let go. Invite the vibrations to rise rather than forcing them — the community refrain is "astral projection happens by itself."
4
Lengthen the exhaleBreathe in 4s · hold 5s · exhale 6–7s · hold after the out-breath. The longer exhale mimics sleep-breathing and shifts the autonomic state. (The 4-7-8 pattern works the same way.)
5
Clear the mental blocksDrop expectation — "wanting" it tightens you and blocks the let-go. Release fear (worrying about "bad spirits" creates exactly the tension that stops you). Highly analytical, left-brain-dominant minds often find the transition harder — you can't think your way in. Avoid alcohol; it dampens the vibrational response.
6
Be patient — and stop chasingTimelines vary wildly: weeks for some, 6–12 months for others, years for a few. A recurring report: the OBE finally arrived after they gave up chasing it. Releasing the chase is itself part of the technique.
7
Consider the lucid-dream routeSeveral practitioners cracked OBEs sideways: "I gave up on OBEs and focused on lucid dreams — once I had them regularly, OBEs came even without trying." Lucid dreaming trains the same body-asleep / mind-awake skill.

The "Oh It's Happening" Reflex

Defusing the Bounce-Out

The single most reliable thing that collapses a short vibrational state — the 2–3 second buzz, the bottom-up numbness, the body-schema shift — is the thought "oh it's happening." The Vibration State section tells you to stay present with it; this is how not to bounce out the instant it arrives.

The skill isn't suppressing the thought — suppression is its own effortful engagement. It's having a pre-loaded, low-energy response ready so the thought passes through instead of catching.

Why it bounces you. "Oh it's happening" re-engages, in one stroke, the entire waking system you just spent the session putting to sleep:

→
Verbal cognition fires back upThe analytical mind you'd quieted — the one that, in a good session, lets Monroe's words become pure sound texture — comes back online to narrate.
→
Emotional spikeExcitement and anticipation trigger a little adrenaline — the opposite of the flat, sleepy neutrality the state needs.
→
Attention turns the sensation into an objectObserving "the vibration" as a thing happening to you splits you into observer + event. That subject/object gap alone is enough to pull you back to the body.

The four moves. Not suppression — substitution. Pre-load each one so there's nothing to "do" when the moment comes.

1
Pre-accept it before you startIn the calm setup phase, decide in advance: "When vibration comes, I do nothing. It's expected. It's allowed to build." Inoculate against novelty — novelty is what triggers the "oh!"
2
Convert noticing into mergingInstead of observer-watching-event, drop attention into the sensation — become the vibration rather than its witness (Buhlman's "intend it to intensify"). No words; lean awareness into the buzz and let it grow. Keeps attention engaged so you don't fall asleep, without splitting into observer/event.
3
Give the mind a neutral, repetitive anchorA steady silent count, or a fixed intention phrase worn smooth ("deeper," "now"). Occupies the verbal channel with something that doesn't excite.
4
Flatten the affectThe tone you want is almost boredom — "oh, this again." The more ordinary the vibrational state feels, the longer it holds. Excitement is the enemy; mild indifference is the friend.

The mouth-noise / exhale note — The involuntary exhale or vocalization at the threshold is the body's motor and breathing control shifting to automatic while a thread of awareness stays online — the same handoff that produces hypnic jerks. It's benign, and is itself a deepening marker, but it can act as a startle trigger that yanks you back. Pre-accept it the same way ("that sound is fine, ignore it") so your own noise doesn't bounce you out.

A competing view — This page treats the vibration as something to merge into and amplify (Buhlman/Monroe). Newport's Lucidology model argues the opposite: vibrations signal you're not yet ready, and the peel-out phase is what wastes them — so he bypasses rather than amplifies. The two routes agree on staying flat and not chasing; they differ only on amplify vs. bypass. See Lucidology → why "books are wrong" about vibrations. Treat it as an open A/B test for your own practice, not a settled answer.

Calibration — Catching the thought as it happens is most of the work; the awareness is already there. The remaining piece is rounding off the reaction. The 2–3 seconds start stretching on their own once the "oh!" stops firing. Carry this into practice as Cue 5 in the 4-Week Protocol.

From the Forums

Practical observations from the Monroe Institute community, r/gatewaytapes, and longtime practitioners.

Setup Before You Start
The temperature drop is realKeep a blanket beside you. Within 20 minutes of deep Focus 10 your body temperature can drop noticeably — people frequently get pulled out of the state by unexpected cold. Have the blanket ready before you start, not after.
Timing matters more than you expectRight after a meal is a near-guarantee of sleep. The sweet spot most people report is 90 minutes after eating, or first thing in the morning after a glass of water. Late-night sessions work for some but tend toward dream-state confusion over clear Focus 10.
Body position is individualThe tapes assume lying down, but a significant number of practitioners do better sitting upright — it reduces the tendency to fall asleep. Experiment. A recliner is often the sweet spot between too alert (chair) and too sleepy (flat bed).
Headphone quality mattersBinaural beats require stereo separation. Earbuds work but over-ear headphones that fully cover the ear canal are consistently reported as producing deeper states. Keep the volume moderate — louder is not more effective.
ECB Energy Conversion Box — What Actually Works
Your box will evolveMost people start with a literal wooden box and find it morphs over weeks — into a safe, a portal, a fire, an ocean. Let it. The form the box takes spontaneously often reflects where you are psychologically. Pay attention to it.
Deposit emotions, not just thoughtsThe manual gives object examples but forum veterans consistently say the ECB is most powerful when you put the emotion itself in — the anxiety, the grief, the anticipation — not just the symbolic object. Feel it leave.
You can return to it mid-sessionIf a thought keeps pulling you back to waking life during the exercise, mentally deposit it. You don't have to restart. This is one of the most underused features of the ECB.
If you have a quiet mind naturallySeveral experienced practitioners say they skipped the ECB entirely after the first few waves — their internal dialogue was quiet enough that the step felt forced. It's a tool, not a ritual requirement.
Tuning Resonant Tuning — The Awkward Phase
Everyone finds it awkward at firstHumming along with voices on a tape, alone in a dark room, feels strange. This phase typically passes by Wave 2. The practitioners who pushed through the self-consciousness consistently report it as one of the most physically effective steps.
What to actually feel forTingling in the skull and chest are the most common physical markers that resonant tuning is working. Some feel it in the sternum, some in the sinus cavity. If you feel nothing, try opening your mouth slightly and varying the vowel sound.
Volume and matchingYou don't need to match the pitch on the tape exactly. Find your own resonant note — the one that vibrates your chest most — and ride it. The goal is vibration in the body, not perfect harmony with the recording.
REBAL REBAL — Common Confusions & What It's Actually Like
Most people visualize it wrong at firstThe most common mistake is treating the REBAL as a static protective bubble you draw around yourself. It's a moving flow — fountain up out of the head, spiral back in through the feet. The movement is the point. Static visualization produces very little.
What it actually feels likeExperienced practitioners describe it variously as: a warm pressure just past the skin, a faint electrical charge at arm's length, a sense of the air being slightly thicker around the body, or a subtle sense of "fullness." Most say it took several weeks of consistent practice before it felt like anything at all.
Popping it in daily lifeThe instruction to activate the REBAL outside of sessions — in crowds, traffic, difficult conversations — is taken seriously by longtime practitioners. Several report it consistently producing a sense of calm detachment and, occasionally, people in the area noticeably orienting toward them.
It recharges you as you recharge itThis is from the source material but worth repeating because practitioners consistently confirm it — the act of building the REBAL itself energizes the practitioner. It's reciprocal, not just outward projection.
Focus 10 What Focus 10 Actually Feels Like
"Mind awake, body asleep" is impreciseMost practitioners report Focus 10 is less like your body being asleep and more like your body becoming irrelevant — it doesn't disappear, you just stop being interested in it. Thoughts are still present but they don't hook you the way they do in waking life.
Common physical markers to look forHeightened heartbeat awareness, unusual skin sensitivity (feeling your clothes or air on skin intensely), temperature shifts in specific areas of the body, phantom sounds (tones, voices, white noise), a sense of the body being very heavy or, paradoxically, very light.
Falling asleep vs. Focus 10The single biggest challenge for most beginners. The practical distinction: in sleep, awareness disappears. In Focus 10, there's a continuous thread of observation even when nothing specific is happening. If you're not sure which you were in, you were probably asleep — and that's fine. Keep going.
Don't judge early sessionsA common forum refrain: "My first 6 sessions felt like nothing." Progress in the Gateway is rarely linear. Sessions that feel flat are often followed by sessions with sudden clarity. Consistency across weeks matters far more than the quality of any single session.
Journal immediately afterThe state leaves fast — like a dream. Keep a notebook or phone beside you. What to note: depth rating, any physical sensations, sounds, images, emotional tone, and anything unusual in daily life in the days that follow. Over time the journal becomes a map.
Body-schema distortion is a strong deepening signA bloated, swollen, oversized, or weightless feeling in the hands or whole body means proprioception is fading and the "body asleep" half is going deeper than usual. Lean into it rather than monitoring it — letting the sensation grow tends to roll forward into vibration.
Verbal-mind drop-outReaching the point where you still hear Monroe's words but no longer process their meaning — words as pure sound texture — is the F10 sweet spot: present but not analytically engaged. Not asleep (you still hear), not narrating.
Brief off-headset vibrationA 2–3 second full-body vibration with the headset removed is the vibrational state proper — the immediate pre-separation signature. Short bursts collapsing is normal and almost always caused by the "oh it's happening" reflex (see the dedicated section above).
