Core Rule: Delta tells you WHERE you are. Gamma tells you HOW FAST you're moving there. Buy option = Long/Positive Gamma. Sell option = Short/Negative Gamma. Always — for both calls and puts.

Vanna & Charm

MMs hedge Vanna and Charm simultaneously with Gamma. These forces move markets even when price hasn't moved at all — explaining moves that look completely random.

Vanna

How much delta expands or shrinks when implied volatility changes. VIX moves → delta shifts across all contracts → MMs re-hedge without price moving.

Long Answer Questions

Write your full answer on paper first. Then reveal the model answer to compare. These require complete explanations — not one-word answers. Cover every step of the chain.

Q1 Retail has been aggressively buying calls for two weeks. VIX sits at 14. A surprise macro shock hits overnight — index gaps down 3% at open. Walk through the COMPLETE chain of events from the initial retail call buying all the way to the VIX spiking to 26. Include Gamma, Vanna, and the Gamma Flip.
// Cover: retail flow → MM gamma → calm market → gap down → call delta → MM re-hedge → gamma flip → Vanna from VIX spike → why VIX spikes further
Retail buys calls aggressively → MMs sell those calls → MMs are SHORT/NEGATIVE gamma → market is calm (VIX 14), index is above gamma flip level → MMs are buying shares to hedge their short calls.
Macro shock hits overnight. Index gaps down 3% at open. Calls that were ATM or slightly OTM are now further OTM. Delta on those calls collapses.
Gamma flow kicks in: MMs were holding too many shares (over-hedged). Must SELL shares to rebalance delta. That selling pushes index lower. Lower index = more delta collapse = more selling needed. Self-reinforcing negative gamma loop.
Gamma Flip triggered: The 3% gap pushes index BELOW the gamma flip level. Now net market GEX is negative. MMs switch from mildly hedging to aggressively amplifying every move. Market character flips from choppy to explosive trending.
Vanna kicks in: The selloff causes VIX to jump (say 14→26). Higher IV means call delta EXPANDS across all contracts. MMs who sold calls are now under-hedged again from Vanna — must buy shares. This creates brief bounces mid-crash. Vanna partially fights the gamma selling.
VIX reaches 26 because: Market expects continued volatility. MM amplification of moves validates those expectations. Institutions start panic-buying puts for protection → MMs short puts → more negative gamma → more amplification → VIX feeds on itself in a reflexive loop.
Final state: Market in full negative gamma territory. Every move amplified. VIX elevated. MMs are simultaneously managing Gamma flows (selling into drops) and Vanna flows (buying from VIX expansion) — two opposing forces creating chaotic, violent price action.
Q2 Explain the difference between delta shrinking (Vanna), delta decaying (Charm), and delta changing (Gamma). Use an analogy for each. Then explain which is most predictable and why this matters for traders.
// Focus on CAUSE of each, not just the result. Then connect predictability to how traders use them.
Gamma — Delta Changes (up or down): Price moves → delta responds immediately and directly. Call delta rises when price rises, falls when price falls. MM re-hedges by buying/selling shares. Analogy: a ball rolling on a slope — steeper slope (higher gamma) = delta accelerates faster. Goes both directions.
Vanna — Delta Shrinks or Expands: VIX/IV changes → delta reprices across the board. Higher IV = market expects bigger moves = delta expands (options more likely to expire ITM). Lower IV = delta shrinks. Price didn't move, time didn't pass — the fear level of the environment changed. Analogy: ice melting faster because the room got warmer. Same ice block, same time — but the temperature (VIX) changed. Goes both directions depending on VIX direction.
Charm — Delta Decays: Time passes → delta slowly drifts toward its terminal value (zero for OTM, 1.0 for ITM). Every day the clock ticks, probability of expiring ITM changes slightly. MM re-hedges daily even if price and VIX are unchanged. Analogy: ice melting because time passed. Room temperature unchanged — just the natural passage of time. Only goes ONE direction — decay. Time cannot reverse.
Predictability: Charm is most predictable — expiry date is fixed, time decay follows a known schedule. Traders can anticipate Monday morning buying, pre-expiry systematic flows. Gamma is second — it follows price, which can be tracked. Vanna is least predictable — VIX can spike any time on any news, creating sudden unexpected re-hedging cascades.
Why this matters: Traders can position AHEAD of Charm flows (systematic buying near expiry) and Gamma flows (buy above flip, short below). But Vanna flows catch everyone off guard — a VIX shock at 2am forces global re-hedging before anyone can react. Understanding which Greek is dominant explains why market moves happen when they do.
Q3 It's Tuesday. Weekly expiry Friday. VIX is 12. GEX chart shows: +$2bn at 5300, +$500m at 5200 (current price), -$1bn at 5100 (gamma flip), -$3bn at 5000. Describe market behavior under two scenarios: (A) index grinds around 5200 all week, (B) surprise geopolitical event drops index to 5050 Wednesday morning.
// Use GEX levels, pinning, landmine, Charm near expiry, gamma flip, VIX relationship
Scenario A — Index grinds near 5200:
5200 is above gamma flip (5100) = positive GEX zone. MMs long gamma. Every dip gets bought, every rally gets sold. Market extremely choppy and suppressed.
5300 acts as a magnet (+$2bn positive GEX). Price likely drifts slowly upward toward 5300 and pins there into Friday expiry — this is Max Pain. Institutions who sold those calls want price to stay below 5300 at expiry.
Charm accelerates Tuesday→Friday. Daily systematic buying from time decay adds a mild upward drift bias each morning. Works in alignment with the magnetic pull toward 5300.
VIX stays suppressed at 12 or drifts lower. Market is boring, mechanical, predictable. Nothing surprising.
Scenario B — Geopolitical shock drops to 5050 Wednesday:
First break: Index crosses 5100 (gamma flip). Immediately switches from positive to negative gamma environment. MMs stop absorbing and start amplifying. Every down tick now chased by more selling.
Approaching 5000 (-$3bn negative GEX landmine): This is the most dangerous level. As price nears 5000, delta on those negative GEX positions shifts violently. MMs forced to short an enormous amount simultaneously. Price doesn't bounce at 5000 — it explodes through.
Near-expiry factor: It's Wednesday before Friday expiry. Gamma is already elevated from approaching expiration. The landmine + near-expiry gamma spike + gamma flip crossing = extreme violence. This is the most dangerous market combination.
Vanna adds to the chaos: The geopolitical shock causes VIX to spike from 12 to 25+. Vanna flows now also re-hedging simultaneously on top of Gamma. Two forces compounding the move.
Same week, same starting position — crossing the gamma flip at 5100 completely changed the market's character from gentle pinning to violent crash.
Q4 A covered call selling program runs for 6 months. Institutions stop selling. Explain the full transition of market character, gamma environment, and VIX over the following weeks. Why is this transition gradual and not instant?
// Think about expiry cycles, how GEX bars shrink over time, what fills the vacuum
During program: Institutions sell calls each month → MMs buy those calls → MMs long/positive gamma → market choppy and suppressed → VIX low (12-15 range) → MMs absorbing every move.
When selling stops: No new call contracts being created on the sell side. Existing live contracts remain in force until expiry. The transition is NOT instant because those existing contracts still require the same hedging until they expire.
Week 1-2 after stopping: Current month's contracts still live. Market still in positive gamma. Behavior unchanged. GEX chart still shows large positive bars at those strike levels.
Monthly OpEx: First expiration after program stops. Large positive GEX bars disappear as contracts expire. Net positive gamma shrinks significantly. MMs no longer holding those long call positions. GEX chart visibly changes — positive bars shrink or disappear.
Subsequent weeks: With no new covered call supply being added, nothing replaces the expired contracts. Net GEX drifts toward neutral then potentially negative if retail/institutions start buying calls instead. VIX begins drifting higher as the shock absorber disappears.
Full transition (1-3 months): Market character completely changes. Choppy, suppressed, predictable behavior gives way to trending, explosive, less predictable moves. VIX gradually rises from suppressed levels to more normal ranges (15-20).
Why gradual: Tied directly to expiry cycles (weekly, monthly, quarterly). Each OpEx removes another layer of the old positive GEX. Traders watching GEX charts see positive bars literally shrinking week by week — the visual signal of the transition happening in slow motion.
Q5 Why are market crashes inherently more violent and faster than rallies? Connect your full answer to: put buying behavior, MM gamma positioning, Vanna during VIX spikes, and the psychological feedback loop between emotion and mechanics.
// This connects emotion to mechanics. Think about WHEN put buying happens relative to when call buying happens.
Timing of put buying vs call buying: Call buying happens gradually during calm, rising markets. Greed builds slowly. Institutions and retail accumulate calls over days and weeks. Put buying happens DURING panic — exactly when markets are already falling fast. Fear is immediate and intense. This timing asymmetry is the root cause of crash violence.
MM gamma positioning during crashes: Surge in put buying → MMs sell puts → MMs short gamma on puts → market drops → put delta grows more negative → MMs must short MORE shares → that shorting pushes index lower → delta grows even more negative → more shorting required. The mechanical cascade is instant and self-reinforcing.
Vanna compounds crashes uniquely: The crash itself causes VIX to spike. That VIX spike triggers Vanna flows on top of Gamma flows simultaneously. Two separate re-hedging cascades happening at the same time, both pushing the market lower (or at least creating extreme volatility). During rallies, Vanna often works AGAINST the rally (VIX drops = delta shrinks = MMs sell). During crashes, both Gamma AND Vanna compound the same direction.
Psychological feedback loop: Falling prices → fear → put buying → MM mechanical shorting → more selling → more fear → more put buying → deeper crash. The emotion and the mechanics are not separate — they feed each other in a reflexive doom loop. There is no equivalent greed loop of this intensity on the upside.
Why rallies are different: Call buying is gradual. Greed builds slowly. MMs can re-hedge incrementally. VIX typically stays low or falls during rallies (meaning Vanna works against the rally). No sudden VIX spike triggering Vanna in the same direction as Gamma. Markets grind up methodically — stairs up, elevator down.
The result: A move that takes weeks to build on the upside can be completely reversed in hours on the downside. The asymmetry is structural — baked into how options mechanics, human psychology, and MM re-hedging all interact during fear vs greed environments.

Market Fear Gauge

Volatility Skew

Skew is the market pricing fear into options. It explains WHY puts cost more than calls — and connects directly to everything you already know about gamma, Vanna, and crash mechanics.

What Skew Is

In theory — a call and put at the same distance from current price should cost the same. In reality, puts are always more expensive. That difference is Skew.

// Example — Index at 5000
4800 Put (OTM) → IV: 22% 5000 ATM → IV: 15% 5200 Call (OTM) → IV: 12%
Same distance from ATM. Put costs nearly DOUBLE the call in implied vol terms.
// Three reasons puts are more expensive
📥
DEMAND

Everyone wants crash protection. Funds, institutions, retail all buy puts as insurance. High demand = higher price.

⚡
CRASH ASYMMETRY

Markets can drop 20% in days. They rarely rally 20% in days. Real asymmetry — options pricing reflects it.

💰
MM RISK PREMIUM

MMs know selling puts is dangerous — crash risk, violent moves, negative gamma. They charge more to take that risk.

Full Picture

Anatomy of a Crash

When a crash hits, it's never just one thing. It's a three-layer pile-on of mechanics, institutional behavior, and retail panic — all pushing the same direction simultaneously.

LAYER 1 — GAMMA MECHANICS

Gamma flip triggers → MMs short gamma → selling into every down tick → self-reinforcing mechanical cascade. No emotion. Pure mathematics.

LAYER 2 — INSTITUTIONAL HEDGING

Funds see losses → buy puts for protection → MMs short those puts → negative gamma on downside → more mechanical shorting. Institutions are rational but their hedging adds fuel.

LAYER 3 — RETAIL PANIC

Retail sees red screens → panic sell shares AND buy puts → more downward pressure + more negative gamma → more selling → more panic. Emotion amplifies the mechanical.

Dead Cat Bounce — Explained

Those violent bounces you see mid-crash? Almost never real buying. Three mechanical forces create them:

ForceMechanismEffect
VannaVIX spike expands call delta → MMs under-hedged → buy sharesViolent upward spike
Delta-1 at expiryDeep ITM puts near expiry — any bounce shifts delta → MMs forced to cover shortsSharp short covering
CharmTime passing shifts delta → systematic buying pressureMild upward drift
Key insight: These bounces look like recovery. They are not. They are mechanical. Once the Vanna/covering pressure exhausts, selling resumes. Don't mistake a dead cat bounce for a trend reversal.
