Market Makers

Delta Hedging

Market Makers (MMs) are on the opposite side of every trade. Their job is not to be directional — they want to stay delta neutral and earn the spread. But to do that, they must continuously re-hedge as price moves.

Who buys MM Position MM Gamma Initial Hedge
Retail/Institution buys calls MM sells calls Negative / Short Buy shares
Institution sells calls (covered) MM buys calls Positive / Long Short shares
Institution buys puts MM sells puts Negative / Short Short shares

Short Gamma = Accelerant. MMs push the market in whichever direction it's already moving. Moves become explosive and trending.
Long Gamma = Shock Absorber. MMs fade every move. Market gets pinned, choppy, mean-reverting.
Q1 Retail is aggressively buying calls. MMs sold those calls. Index rallies. What do MMs do?
A Sell shares — cushioning the rally
B Buy shares — amplifying the rally
✓ Correct! MMs short gamma → delta rises on rally → under-hedged → buy shares → rally amplifies.
✗ Wrong. MMs are short gamma here. As index rises, they must BUY shares to re-hedge, amplifying the move.
Q2 Institutions are selling covered calls. MMs bought those calls. Index drops. What happens to the index?
A Drop gets cushioned — MMs buy shares
B Drop accelerates — MMs sell shares
✓ Correct! MMs long gamma → delta shrinks on drop → over-hedged → buy shares → drop cushioned.
✗ Wrong. MMs bought calls = Long Gamma. As index drops, delta shrinks → they buy shares → cushions the drop.

What Happens at Expiration

With massive ATM call open interest near expiry and MMs short gamma, two outcomes are possible:

OUTCOME A
Price Pins at Strike

MMs buy dips and sell rips right at the strike. Price gets magnetically stuck. This is Max Pain.

OUTCOME B
Explosive Breakout

Price breaks away from strike. MMs must chase aggressively. Gamma spike creates a violent, fast move.

Q1 MMs are short gamma on calls. Index surges and calls go deep ITM. What happens to MM buying pressure?
A Buying stops — rally loses fuel
B Buying accelerates even more
✓ Correct! Deep ITM → delta near 1.0 → gamma collapses → MMs fully hedged → buying pressure stops.
✗ Wrong. Deep ITM = delta maxed at ~1.0 = gamma collapses. MMs are already fully hedged, so buying STOPS.
Q2 Massive ATM call OI sitting at 5200. Tomorrow is expiry. Index is at 5200. What happens?
A Index definitely rallies through 5200
B Index definitely crashes below 5200
C Either pins at 5200 OR explosive move — both possible
✓ Correct! Both outcomes are possible — pinning (Max Pain) OR explosive breakout if price moves away from the strike.
✗ Wrong. Both outcomes are possible at expiry. Price can pin at the strike OR explode through it depending on which way it breaks first.

Gamma Flip: The price level where MMs shift from net long to net short gamma (or vice versa). Above it → calm market. Below it → explosive market. VIX spikes when this flip occurs.

Gamma Exposure

GEX Charts

GEX (Gamma Exposure) is the total dollar gamma MMs must hedge across all open contracts at each strike. It shows exactly where hedging pressure is concentrated.

GEX Formula

GEX = Open Interest × Gamma × Contract Size × Spot Price

= Total dollars MMs must buy or sell per 1% move at each strike

Positive GEX

Acts as a magnet. Price gets pulled and pinned. MMs sell rips and buy dips around it. Common expiry pinning level.

Negative GEX

Acts as a landmine. Price approaches calmly, then explodes through. MMs forced to chase with massive hedging flows.

The Golden Rule: Above gamma flip = Trade Mean Reversion. Below gamma flip = Trade Momentum.
Q1 Price is above the gamma flip level. VIX is 13. What's your trading approach?
A Mean reversion — fade every move
B Momentum — follow every breakout
✓ Correct! Above flip = positive GEX = MMs dampening moves = fade them.
✗ Wrong. Above gamma flip = positive GEX environment = market gets suppressed = mean reversion wins.
Q2 Largest negative GEX bar sits at 5000 strike. Price approaches 5000. What do you expect?
A Price pins at 5000 and stalls
B Price explodes through 5000 — landmine triggered
✓ Correct! Largest negative GEX = landmine. MMs forced to hedge massively → explosive move through it.
✗ Wrong. Negative GEX = landmine, not magnet. Pinning happens at POSITIVE GEX strikes.

How much delta changes as time passes. Every day, options decay → delta shifts → MMs re-hedge daily just from time.

Most powerful in last 2–3 days before expiration. Creates systematic Monday morning buying drift.

Q1 Market drifts slowly higher on Monday morning. No news, no VIX move. What's driving it?

✓ Correct! No price catalyst, no VIX move = Charm. Weekend time decay shifted delta → MMs buy back shares Monday morning.
✗ Wrong. This is Charm — time passed over the weekend shifting delta on all contracts. MMs systematically re-hedge Monday morning.
Q2 VIX spikes from 14 to 28 overnight. MMs are short calls. What do Vanna flows force MMs to do?

B Sell shares — crashing the market

✗ Wrong. VIX spike → IV rises → call delta increases → MMs need to BUY shares to re-hedge. Vanna can actually SUPPORT markets during vol spikes.
